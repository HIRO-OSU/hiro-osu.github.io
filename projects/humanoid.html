Project detail

Humanoid Actuator Development

Before we can build a capable humanoid platform, we are engineering cost-effective actuators that mix high-power drone motors with advanced 3D printed cycloidal gearboxes.

Goal

Create compact, high-torque, low-backlash joints for future HIRO humanoids.

Strategies

Drone motors, additive manufacturing, and composite cycloidal plates with embedded sensing.

Inspiration

OpenTorque open hardware actuator ecosystem plus industrial cobot best practices.

Meet the actuation pod Learn from OpenTorque

Mission

Accessible actuators, scalable humanoid hardware.

Custom actuators are often the most expensive and time-consuming part of a humanoid build. We are redesigning the stack with commodity drone motors, PAHT-CF prints, and test coupons in PPA-CF to identify the lightest, strongest cycloidal stages for our humanoid joints while learning from OpenTorque's nylon planetary baseline and translating those lessons into our cycloidal layout.

Motor selection

Benchmarking drone outrunners for torque density and thermal performance.

Gearbox experiments

Evaluating PAHT-CF today and considering PPA-CF for future higher-temp tooth profiles.

Embedded sensing

Integrating low-cost encoders and temperature sensors for closed-loop control.

Roadmap

Our roadmap progresses from torque cells to limb assemblies, ensuring every iteration feeds into the eventual HIRO humanoid.
