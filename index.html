Who we are

Hands-on robotics for curious minds.

We are a collective of Oregon State students who want to apply classroom knowledge to ambitious robotics challenges. Our members span firmware, controls, embedded AI, mechanical design, and product storytelling. Together we prototype humanoid limbs, build sensor stacks, and explore autonomous behaviors.

Build & iterate

From CAD to code, members move quickly and learn by doing. We document each sprint so future cohorts can accelerate.

Share knowledge

Members document every sprint, publish build notes, and teach one another through small group sessions.

Engage community

We showcase prototypes at campus club fairs and engineering showcases to inspire the next wave of roboticists.

Field Deployment

SO101 Arm · Visual Language Action Model

Deploying and testing NVIDIA's GR00T 1.5 VLA alongside a local VLM on Jetson Thor so the SO101 arm can autonomously play tic-tac-toe.

Hardware R&D

HIRO Humanoid Actuators

Building cost-efficient actuators with drone motors, PAHT-CF cycloidal gearboxes, and upcoming PPA-CF tests before scaling to a full humanoid platform.
